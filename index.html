Meet Leng Cheaheng — Born and Raised in the Shadow of Angkor

Hello — I'm Leng Cheaheng, and Siem Reap is not just where I live. It is who I am. I grew up surrounded by the ancient stones of Angkor, listening to my grandparents tell stories of the Khmer kings, the temple builders, and the spirit of a people who created one of the greatest civilisations the world has ever known.

For over 12 years I have had the privilege of sharing that world with travellers from every corner of the globe. Every morning I wake up genuinely excited to show someone the sunrise over Angkor Wat for the first time — to watch their face when the golden light hits the reflection pool and they realise they are standing in front of something truly extraordinary.

This work is my passion, but it is also how I care for the people I love most. I drive every day to support my wonderful wife, our daughter, and our soon-to-be-born son. When I guide a family through the temples, I think about my own family — about the Cambodia I want my children to be proud of, and the stories I want them to inherit. That is why I never treat a tour as just a job. Every journey is a chance to share my culture, my heritage, and my home.

I speak fluent English and I know every road, every hidden path, and every local restaurant that the guidebooks miss. My car is clean, air-conditioned, and always on time. But more than that — I will be your friend in Siem Reap.

The Wonders of Angkor

Must Visit

Angkor Wat

The largest religious monument in the world. I recommend arriving at 4:45 AM to secure the best spot for the sunrise reflection in the lotus pond. We'll explore the intricate bas-reliefs while most tourists are still at breakfast.

The Stone Faces

Bayon Temple

Located in the heart of Angkor Thom, Bayon is famous for the 216 giant smiling faces of Avalokiteshvara. The morning light here creates dramatic shadows on the weathered sandstone, perfect for photography.

Nature's Embrace

Ta Prohm

Left largely in the state it was found, Ta Prohm is where the jungle and history collide. Massive silk-cotton trees wrap their roots around ancient walls, creating a "Tomb Raider" atmosphere unlike anywhere else.
